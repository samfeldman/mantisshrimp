Facts

Some things you might like to know

  • Mantis shrimp have 16 color-receptive cones in their eyes, leading to an unimaginable (from a human perspective) number of color combinations they can see.
  • Mantis shrimp use raptorial appendages on the front of their bodies to strike prey with 1,500 Newtons of force, or, through the process of super-cavitation (ridiculously quick boiling) create a shockwave that stuns their prey.
  • Mantis shrimp exoskeletons are so strong scientists have been studying them to develop body armor for people.
  • Mantis shrimp have to be housed in special, super-strong aquariums by themselves or they will slaughter any other marine animals inside and break the glass.
